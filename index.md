I am a Senior Genomic Surveillance Data Scientist at the Wellcome Sanger Institute, where I work on large-scale pathogen genomics, statistical modelling, and infectious disease analysis for public health decision-making.

I completed my PhD in Biological Sciences at the University of Cambridge (2018-2023), where I developed computational methods for modelling antimicrobial resistance dynamics in *Streptococcus pneumoniae*. My current work spans antimalarial resistance surveillance, meta-analysis at population scale, and reproducible bioinformatics pipeline development.

My research interests sit at the intersection of pathogen evolution, genomic epidemiology, quantitative modelling, and translational data science. I am especially interested in projects that combine rigorous statistical inference with practical impact in global health.
