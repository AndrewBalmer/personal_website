About

I am a Senior Lecturer in Sociology at the University of Manchester and a member of the Morgan Centre for Research into Everyday Lives.

My research sits at the intersection of the sociology of everyday life, the sociology of science and technology, and the development of creative research methods. I completed my PhD at the Institute for Science and Society, University of Nottingham, and have held posts at Sheffield and Nottingham before joining Manchester.

I am happy to supervise PhD students with interests in lying, deception and secrecy; lie detection and surveillance technology; sociology of everyday lives; and creative research methods.

Research

🔎

Sociology of Everyday Life

Concerned with lying, deception, secrecy and information management in routine social interactions. Current work explores the sociological potential of the Mass Observation Project to study everyday life across time and social contexts.

🌟

Creative Qualitative Methods

Development and application of creative methods for social science research and public engagement with science. Including sensory, performative, and arts-based approaches to studying everyday experience.

🔬

Sociology of Scientific Practices

Ethnographic and qualitative studies of scientific work, focusing on synthetic biology and dementia research. Examining how scientists, engineers and governance actors negotiate emerging biotechnologies.

Books

Lie Detection and the Law: Torture, Technology and Truth

Andrew Balmer — Routledge

A history and sociology of lie detection technology, from trial by ordeal through the polygraph to neuroimaging, exploring how conceptualisations of the body and truth have been invested in detection practices.

Synthetic Biology: A Sociology of Changing Practices

Andrew Balmer, Katie Bulpin & Susan Molyneux-Hodgson — Palgrave Macmillan, 2016

An ethnographic exploration of the emergence of synthetic biology, examining how academics, students, industrialists and governance actors tried to bring engineering and biology together.

The Craft of Writing in Sociology

Andrew Balmer — Manchester University Press

A guide to academic writing for sociologists, addressing craft, voice and the challenges of communicating sociological ideas.
